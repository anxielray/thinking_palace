The Quantum Twist: Understanding the Strange Side of Physics

By Raymond Martin • May 8, 2025

Quantum Mechanics • Physics • Mathematical Insight

Quantum mechanics defies our understanding of reality. Where classical physics sees a ball rolling down a hill, quantum theory introduces a particle that exists in multiple places at once, only "collapsing" into one when observed.

This article will explore the fundamentals behind wave-particle duality, Schrödinger’s cat, quantum entanglement, and how these theories are not just mental games—but crucial components of how the universe behaves.

1. The Principle of Superposition

In quantum theory, particles can be in multiple states simultaneously. This is known as superposition. When we observe a system, the superposition collapses into a single, observable state.

“The most incomprehensible thing about the universe is that it is comprehensible.” — Albert Einstein

2. Entanglement & Spooky Action

When two particles become entangled, the state of one instantly affects the other—even across vast distances. This phenomenon puzzled even Einstein, who famously called it "spooky action at a distance".
